GoSafeChanging the way women think, live and commute



Introduction

Globally, at least 35% of women experience physical or sexual violence. Several research studies indicate that sexual assault against women is a global problem and not limited to developing countries. According to the research done by the non profit- Stop Street Harassment in the US, 37% do not feel safe walking home at night, while 65% have faced street harassment. According to Actionaid reports (2014), more than 40% women feel unsafe in public places where young men gather. In developing countries like Vietnam, 87% women face sexual violence, while in India it is 79%. Men ercieve that public places are run by them, making it a reason to harass women. When women do not feel safe on the streets, it becomes a reason to stay at home or drop out school early.



How is it different ?

As the staggering numbers keep increasing, the existing safety products like pepper sprays, personal alarms and stun guns fail to provide safety due to its practical limitations. For instance, while safety alarms provide some sense of security to women, by informing their loved ones, they do not help them defend themselves during the attack. Pepper sprays, on the other hand pose the risk of self-victimization, due to winds and when the attacker overpowers the victim. During design research it was found that panic negatively affects women in reacting quickly and defend themselves in close encounter scenarios.



Product Development

GoSafe was designed as part of the Advanced Concept Design course (Industrial Design Engineering Faculty) at TU Delft. The product is based on Optical Incapacitation, flash blinding and disorienting an attacker up to one minute, giving enough time for the user to escape. The trigger was designed to be based on reflexive use cues and activates at the clench of the fist. This also enables greater degree of freedom of hands in defensive moves.

GoSafe comes in two flavors- Everyday wear (unisex-fitness wearable) and a trendy fashion wear- both of which features concealed Optical Incapacitators. This way the attacker is unaware of the wearable. Thanks to the product architecture, GoSafe become unusable when snatched away (to prevent self-victimization).


The companion app provides extended safety features to the user, however, the product functions independently to avoid risks, like a dead battery (in smartphones). The app architecture is inspired by the Uber app model, where both the user and the nearest police share with each other their location.




User Tests

Tests were conducted using a defender- attacker experimental setup, at TU Delft. The attackers reported being flash blinded up to 2 minutes especially when used in the dark. User interviews were conducted in non-profits in India who also provided positive feedback in the test results.




MIT Bootcamp 2018

GoSafe was further developed during the MIT Innovation and Entrepreneurship Bootcamp 2018 at Rio de Janerio. A business model was also constructed around the model during the one week of Bootcamp. A primary market research was developed with Sao Paulo as the Beach head Market



A quick prototype was constructed for customer journey mapping and interactions with real people, who ranged from pedestrians, the venue staff and women taxi drivers. The interactions not only gave valuable insights but also some amazing friednships.

The interactions not only gave valuable insights but also made some good friendships. The chef team who took efforts to feed me amazing vegan food, were very glad with the product developed. The female cook expressed how the product could help her define the way she experiences commuting in Rio.


The project was highly appreciated by the Bootcamp coaches and gave me the opportunity to deliver a speech on the closing day that won a standing ovation.




Inspiration

The idea was inspired from tragic event in 1997, when hundreds of children across Japan were hospitalized when a Pokemon episode (called the "Electric Soldier Porygon") was broadcasted. Some of them passed out, experienced blurred vision and in some extreme cases some they even experienced seizures. It was caused due to the rapid strobing lights of Red and blue in one scene (Stroboscopic Effect).
